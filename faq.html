Product Information

What is the difference between travertine and marble? +

Travertine and marble are both beautiful natural stones, but they have distinct characteristics:

  • Travertine: A form of limestone with a more porous surface and natural holes. It has a warmer, more rustic appearance and is excellent for both interior and exterior use. It's typically more affordable than marble.
  • Marble: A metamorphic rock with a more refined, elegant appearance. It has a smoother surface with distinctive veining patterns. Marble is often used for luxury applications and requires more maintenance than travertine.

Both stones offer unique beauty and can be sealed to enhance durability and ease of maintenance.

Is granite better than marble for kitchen countertops? +

Granite and marble each have their advantages:

  • Granite: Extremely durable, heat-resistant, and less prone to scratching or staining. It's ideal for high-traffic kitchens and is easier to maintain.
  • Marble: Offers unmatched elegance and beauty but requires more maintenance. It can be more susceptible to staining and etching from acidic substances.

For busy kitchens, granite is often the preferred choice due to its durability. However, marble can be an excellent choice if you're willing to maintain it properly and appreciate its unique beauty.

Can natural stone be used outdoors? +

Yes, many natural stones are suitable for outdoor use, but some are better than others:

  • Travertine: Excellent for outdoor patios, pool decks, and walkways
  • Granite: Highly durable and weather-resistant, perfect for outdoor applications
  • Limestone: Can be used outdoors but may require more maintenance
  • Marble: Generally not recommended for outdoor use in harsh climates

Proper sealing and installation are crucial for outdoor stone applications. Our team can advise you on the best stone choice for your specific outdoor project.

What is onyx and what makes it special? +

Onyx is a rare and luxurious natural stone known for its translucent properties and dramatic veining patterns. When backlit, onyx creates stunning visual effects, making it perfect for:

  • Feature walls and accent pieces
  • Backlit bar fronts and countertops
  • Decorative panels and artistic installations
  • Luxury bathroom applications

Onyx is more delicate than granite or marble and requires careful handling and installation. It's an excellent choice for creating dramatic focal points in luxury spaces.

How do I maintain and care for natural stone? +

Proper maintenance is essential for preserving the beauty of natural stone:

  • Regular Cleaning: Use pH-neutral stone cleaners and avoid acidic or abrasive products
  • Sealing: Most natural stones benefit from periodic sealing to protect against stains
  • Spill Management: Clean spills immediately, especially acidic substances like wine or citrus
  • Protection: Use coasters, placemats, and trivets to protect surfaces from heat and scratches
  • Professional Maintenance: Periodic professional cleaning and resealing may be recommended

We provide detailed care instructions specific to each stone type with your purchase.

How often should natural stone be sealed? +

The frequency of sealing depends on the stone type and usage:

  • Granite: Typically every 1-3 years, depending on usage
  • Marble: Every 6-12 months for high-traffic areas, annually for others
  • Travertine: Every 1-2 years, more frequently for outdoor applications
  • Limestone: Every 1-2 years
  • Onyx: Requires careful sealing, typically every 6-12 months

You can test if resealing is needed by placing a few drops of water on the surface. If it darkens or absorbs quickly, it's time to reseal.
